Hello! Here's a bit about me

Originally from near Philly, I am now a second-year student at Georgia Tech and a member of our Honors Program. I am majoring in computer science and minoring in both math and economics.

I am passionate about social sciences, and am captivated by complex systems, such as political institutions, capital markets, and media environments, are able to transform our individual lives. Focusing on developing strong quantitative skills to apply towards interdisciplinary research, I aim to inform public policy, guide social enterprises, and more.

My academic ambitions

I am currently a sophomore at the Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta, GA. As a member of the Honors Program, I am a motivated student with a 4.0 GPA pursuing a rigorous course load. I love to learn, and will likely continue my education in graduate school. I am pursuing three degrees for when I graduate in the spring of 2023:

  1. Computer Science Major

    So far, I have built a strong programming foundation with object oriented programming, data structures, and algorithms. Now, I am beginning my upper-level coursework where I am concentrating in Intelligence and Theory.

    With the Intelligence thread, I am exploring human cognitive processes through psychology courses, and learning how to apply these concepts with artificial intelligence and machine learning.

    Through my Theory classes, I am investigating the mathematical underpinnings of computer science, enabling me to develop more sophisticated and efficient algorithms.

  2. Mathematics Minor

    Taking a tough, proofs based class helped me see math as more than a tedious series of arithmetic and calculations. As I began to understand how math reflects complex systems in our world and models decisions in our lives, I decided to take on this minor.

    Paired with my Theory thread, this degree will help me build strong analytical skills that will enable me to work with data in any capacity.

  3. Economics Minor

    Economics is a bridge that allows me to apply my quantitative skills to analyze complex social systems. Through this lens, I hope to use advanced computational methods from computer science and math to advance research in social science fields.
